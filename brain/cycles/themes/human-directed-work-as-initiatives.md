# Hand-directed work routed through the pipeline blurs autonomy metrics

To get trafficGame structurally tractable the operator queued
`INIT-2026-05-10-trafficgame-simplification-{tests,source,arch}` —
deliberate, human-authored structural surgery (rip-and-redraw the test
suite, extract god-files) — and **ran it through the forge pipeline as
if it were autonomous product work**. The seven `2026-05-10-*` brain
snapshot themes were likewise **hand-authored**, substituting for the
reflector to close the project out.

This is the operator's "blurred lines": the moment the goal stopped
being "observe forge progress the project unattended" and became "use
forge as a delivery vehicle for specific work I need done." Both are
legitimate, but they have **different success criteria** — autonomous
cycles are judged on convergence-without-intervention; hand-directed
cycles are judged on whether the specified work landed. The queue and
`forge metrics` record them identically, so the autonomy signal is
polluted: a hand-driven simplification that needed constant operator
patching reads, in the metrics, like an autonomous success.

Lesson: distinguish the two modes structurally. Remediation (closure
goal G6): every manifest declares `origin: architect | human-directed`;
the reflector and metrics separate the cohorts so "did forge get more
autonomous" is answerable. Without the tag, every reliability claim
about forge is confounded by the close-out work that produced it.
